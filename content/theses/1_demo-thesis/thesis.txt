Thesisabstract: Web design is a critical discipline at the intersection of aesthetics, usability, and technology, shaping how users experience the digital world. This thesis explores the evolution of web design principles, focusing on user experience (UX), responsive design, and accessibility. Through a mixed-methods approach, it examines current trends, tools, and frameworks, highlighting the shift from static, desktop-oriented sites to dynamic, mobile-first designs. Key topics include the role of minimalist design, the impact of color psychology, and the integration of interactive elements to boost engagement. The research addresses challenges faced by designers, such as balancing creativity with usability and ensuring compatibility across diverse devices and browsers. Case studies of leading websites illustrate best practices, while user testing provides insight into effective design choices.
